Why I made this project

I wanted to take on the challenge of making my first HTML template and making it available initially for free for anybody who might need such a template for a project. I thought a template for a SAAS product would be more interesting but decided to go with accountancy as I know of a potential client who may need one in the near future.

Research

Process

I looked at all the various platforms selling templates and found that there weren’t that many for accountancy. They aren’t probably the most popular but the few good ones I saw had really good sales numbers. Business templates in general are among the most popular on these market places.

Idea

I had the idea of having an accountancy firm helping small businesses with their accounting needs. Many firms struggle with keeping up with their paperwork which can result in stressful situations. I help businesses with their web presence so it’s similar to what I do but taken from an accounting perspective.

Persona

I created a persona based on a potential client of this accountancy firm, a small business owner.

Goals

  • File taxes easily
  • Reduce stress by keeping books clean
  • Having predictable finances and less worry mean more time to grow their business

Frustrations

  • Too busy to keep up
  • Overwhelmed by never-ending paperwork
  • May be worrying about penalties if they pay too little tax or paying too much tax

About Samantha

Age: 32

Business: Restaurant owner

Location: London

Meet Samantha, a 32-year-old London based restaurant owner. Samantha has always had a strong interest in cuisine and decided to turn her passion into a career by opening her own restaurant. She has, however, had little time to devote to her accounting needs due to the demands of the day-to-day running of her business.

Samantha's busy restaurant schedule puts strain on her accounting responsibilities. She wants to simplify her accounting process so that she can have more time to focus on growing her company.

High-Fidelity UI Design

Once the initial wireframes and flows were complete, I began by designing the main landing page, establishing fonts and colors.

Look & feel

To find the right styles, I find listing brand values help me guide me in the right direction. Accountancy falls into the clean, simple and neutral category of websites, the brand values that determined this are:

  • reliable
  • professional
  • efficient
  • trustworthy
  • detail-oriented
  • clean
  • serious
  • balanced

The brand conveys professionalism, trustworthiness and seriousness and this needs to be expressed in the design.

Font

Public Sans

Regular, Medium, Semibold and Bold

AaBbCcDdEdFfGgHh

As the brand values have one common ‘feel’ one font will suffice. It has to be a sans serif font as a serif font is more suited for friendlier, luxurious and more formal brands which this is not. Open sans also has a modern look and feel and it is easy to read which makes it well-suited for use in a business context.

Color palette

The following color palette was used, from left to right: Accent, primary background, secondary background, secondary background and text, text on light background and error message.

For this type of business these are common brand colors which I normally would try to stay away from. Having different colors from the competition should make you stand out from the crowd. This is a template so whoever will use it can change the colors to suit their own branding.

Accessibility Check

This template has been evaluated for contrast to match the AA standards of WCAG.

Original colors chosen:

WCAG AA: Fail

AA compliant colors:

WCAG AA: Pass

My originally chosen colors have a more neutral feel. When I added white text on top of them, they both failed the AA standards. I then had a choice of making a darker variation of each of the colors or to add a dark text on top instead. I don’t think dark text would work well so I made the colors darker and richer instead.
