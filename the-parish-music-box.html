The Context And Challenge

Key points of my design process:

Project background and description

  • The Parish Music Box is a band formed in London in 2006. They are best described as a lounge beat combo which is all about the melody, place and story.
  • Inspired by The Smiths, Lambchop and Tindersticks, they released a critically acclaimed debut, “Paradise Is Pocket Sized” some years ago and have ever since been releasing new music and playing the London pub scene and clubs.

The problem - the 'why?' and the plan

  • They came to me as they wanted a showcase or a website for potential bookers to see and hear them.
  • Their main issue was that as a band they enjoy performing and they would like to do more of it!
  • A landing page that ads or social networks can link to could help with this.

Project goals and objectives

  • A website to gather all their info and content in one place.
  • If it’s easier to find out who they are the chances of getting more bookings will increase.
  • They would then play more often which is good revenue for the venues as they already have a following.

Brand and UI Design

Branding, or what you want your users to feel about you, can be reflected in the typography you choose. For this project we came up with the following brand adjectives:

  • Creative    Earthy    Elegant    Classy
  • Vintage    Clean    Humble    Reflective
  • Retro    Human    Intelligent    Well-Crafted

With the brand in place I needed the typography to show different aspects of the brand. I decided on the following two fonts:

Playfair Display

  • I wanted a font that looked similar to the font in the logo and this one matches well!
  • It gives off the vintage/retro feel to it which represents the band.
  • It’s a modern serif and only applied to the headings.

Fira Sans

  • Fira Sans for any other text.
  • Fira Sans I feel represents a clean and simple vibe but with hints of human, warmth and elegance which is what the band is about.
  • Humanist Sans serif font that feels simple and clean.
  • It works well as a good body font as it got a high X-height, got all the weights and Itallics.

In the above screenshot, the headline is Playfair Display and Fira Sans as the body text.

Medium brand, how did I decide which fonts to use?

  • This band’s brand is more than neutral as on the one hand they represent elegance, humanity and warmth, but on the other hand they also have a strong vintage and retro vibe to their brand.
  • The website is suited for a medium brand;some branding but no need to ‘overdo’ it with too many textures or too much character as you see in high branded products.
  • To achieve a medium branded website I needed two fonts that matched each other more than two that don’t.
  • A good way to test this is to match skeletons of the two fonts to see if they have similar overall outline, shapes and letterforms:

Screenshot of the skeletons of the two fonts placed on top of each other:

Fira Sans put on top of Playfair Display. Both aligned to the baseline and the x-height. I adjusted the letterspacing of Fira Sans so you can compare them more easily.

Conclusion

  • On closer examination you can see the the basics shapes of both are quite similar with short descenders, slightly condensed letterforms and a large x-height.
  • The skeletons of both match well so they would definitely fit into the category of medium branding. If they didn’t match the brand for this pairing would be higher.

Annotations

When I start a project I always ask myself what is the purpose of the design. A way of defining a hierarchy that will guide the user to hopefully push a button is to annotate or add strips that help define the individual user journey visually from top to bottom.

It's also a good way to make sure a particular design contains everything it should.

Please see below for more information about what each strip contains:

  • Emotion

    Humans are visual creatures, so often emotional connection comes in the form of an image. An image usually hooks the user and hence will strongly influence their decision. A great image of the Parish Music Box playing live should peek a user's interest here!

  • Description

    It should give you a validation of the emotional first impression. First confirmation in the decision making process.

  • Details

    Will give users more information to clear any doubts they may have. The description and the details sections should be asking or answering the question, is this for me? In this case, is this a band I want to book or check out?

  • Clarify

    This could be user reviews that will strongly influence the user to make a final decision. If it's good for others it should be good for me!

  • Action

    Once the user reaches this stage, the user should know everything he or she needs to make the final decision. Hopefully any user here will book the band or see them live!
